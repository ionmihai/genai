GenAI Tools for Teaching

NotebookLM

NotebookLM is Google’s AI-powered research and note-taking tool. Upload your course materials (PDFs, docs, slides, websites) and NotebookLM can generate:

  • Audio Overviews (“Podcasts”): Two AI hosts discuss your content in a conversational style. Choose from Deep Dive, Brief (1-2 min), Critique, or Debate formats.
  • Flashcards: Customizable study cards for key terms, dates, and concepts with adjustable difficulty levels.
  • Quizzes: Multiple-choice questions with optional hints and explanations.

Teaching use: Convert dense lecture notes or readings into engaging podcast-style summaries students can listen to on the go, or auto-generate study aids for exam prep.

ChatGPT Projects

ChatGPT Projects are smart workspaces that organize chats, files, and custom instructions together. Upload up to 20-40 files (depending on plan) including PDFs, spreadsheets, images, and documents—ChatGPT references these across all conversations in the project.

  • Supports files up to 512MB and 2M tokens
  • Built-in memory retains context across sessions
  • Can synthesize, transform, and extract information from complex documents

Teaching use: Upload teaching materials into a “Project” and ChatGPT can then answer questions with citations, explain derivations, or (for teachers) help you identify gaps in your materials. Particularly strong for STEM content with equations and figures that other tools struggle to parse (NotebookLM is not as good at this).

Gemini in Chrome

Gemini in Chrome is Google’s new browser-native AI integration. Access Gemini directly from Chrome’s sidebar to interact with web content.

  • Ask Gemini to clarify complex information across up to 10 open tabs simultaneously
  • Get key takeaways and summaries from webpages
  • Use Gemini Live to have natural voice conversations while browsing
  • AI Mode in the address bar for quick questions

Teaching use: If your lecture notes, readings, or course materials are hosted online (Canvas pages, course websites, online textbooks), students can ask Gemini questions directly about the content without copy-pasting. Useful for navigating and understanding multi-page web-based course materials. Make sure students understand that this should not be used on webpages that contain private information

Claude in Excel

Claude in Excel is Anthropic’s native Excel add-in (currently in beta for Claude Pro, Max, Team, and Enterprise users). Access Claude from a sidebar within Excel or through Microsoft 365 Copilot’s Agent Mode.

  • Reads multi-tab spreadsheets with cell-level citations
  • Debugs and fixes formulas while preserving dependencies
  • Creates pivot tables and charts
  • Builds new spreadsheets from scratch or populates templates
  • Tracks and explains all changes with full transparency

Teaching use: For courses involving data analysis, Claude can help students understand complex spreadsheet logic, debug formula errors, and learn Excel workflows interactively. Instructors can use it to quickly build example datasets or grade spreadsheet-based assignments.

OpenAI Prism

OpenAI Prism is a free AI-native workspace for scientific writing. It’s a LaTeX-native editor powered by GPT-5.2 designed for researchers and educators.

  • Draft, compile, and collaborate in real-time with unlimited collaborators
  • AI-assisted writing with full document context
  • Search and incorporate literature from arXiv directly
  • Convert whiteboard equations or diagrams into LaTeX
  • Automated proofreading, citation management, and equation formatting

Teaching use: Create professional lecture slides and course materials with proper mathematical typesetting. The AI can help refine explanations, suggest relevant literature, and ensure consistent formatting. Free for anyone with a ChatGPT personal account—making it accessible for students writing technical reports or theses.

VS Code + Codex Extension

OpenAI Codex is a VS Code extension that functions as an AI coding agent with full access to your workspace files. Included with ChatGPT Plus, Pro, Business, Edu, and Enterprise plans.

  • Side-by-side mode: Chat with Codex in a panel while it reads and edits your files with full context
  • Cloud delegation: Offload larger tasks to run in the background
  • Accepts text, screenshots, or diagrams as input
  • Generates, debugs, explains, and refactors code

Teaching use: Coding agents like Codex are good at more than just coding. This gives LLMs direct access to your local files so you don’t have to copy-paste anything into ChatGPT. You can just ask Codex to read, edit, create new files on your computer. Students can use it as a tutoring tool that understands their entire project context.
